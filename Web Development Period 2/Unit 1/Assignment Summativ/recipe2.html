Masala Mac & Cheese with Tandoori Cauliflower

A comforting American classic meets the boldness of Indian spices. This creamy baked macaroni and cheese is elevated with smoky tandoori-roasted cauliflower and a hint of garam masala in the sauce.

Perfect for when you want something indulgent and familiar, but with a bold, spiced twist. The roasted cauliflower brings texture and smokiness, while the masala cheese sauce ties it all together into a dish that’s both hearty and flavorful.

For the Tandoori Cauliflower

  • 300 g cauliflower florets
  • 2 tbsp yogurt
  • 1 tsp red chili powder
  • ½ tsp turmeric powder
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • ½ tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp oil

For the Masala Cheese Sauce

  • 3 tbsp butter
  • 3 tbsp all-purpose flour
  • 3 cups milk (warm)
  • 2 cups cheddar cheese, grated
  • 1 cup mozzarella, grated
  • 1 tsp garam masala
  • 1 tsp cumin powder
  • 1 tsp Kashmiri chili powder (optional, for color)
  • ½ tsp black pepper
  • Salt to taste

For the Pasta

  • 300 g elbow macaroni (or any short pasta)
  • 1 tbsp salt for boiling water

Garnish

  • Chopped coriander leaves
  • Bread crumbs (optional, for crunchy topping)

How To Prepare:

  1. Prep the Cauliflower
    1. Preheat oven to 200°C (390°F).
    2. Mix yogurt, spices, salt, lemon juice, and oil in a bowl.
    3. Toss in cauliflower florets and coat evenly.
    4. Spread on a baking tray and roast for 20–25 minutes until slightly charred.
  2. Cook the Pasta
    1. Boil pasta in salted water until al dente.
    2. Drain and set aside.
  3. Make the Masala Cheese Sauce
    1. Melt butter in a saucepan. Add flour and cook for 1–2 minutes.
    2. Slowly whisk in warm milk until smooth.
    3. Add cheddar and mozzarella, stirring until melted.
    4. Mix in garam masala, cumin, chili powder, black pepper, and salt.
  4. Combine & Bake
    1. Mix cooked pasta and tandoori cauliflower into the masala cheese sauce.
    2. Transfer to a baking dish. Top with breadcrumbs and extra cheese if desired.
    3. Bake at 180°C (350°F) for 15 minutes until golden and bubbly.
  5. Garnish & Serve
    1. Sprinkle with coriander leaves.
    2. Serve hot with garlic naan or as a stand-alone indulgent dish.
